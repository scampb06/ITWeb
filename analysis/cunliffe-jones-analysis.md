# For these specific attacks please examine the Excel spreadsheet and your own external sources to determine who was behind these false claims and what their motivations were

Based on the Excel spreadsheet analysis, the actors behind these harmful false claims and their motivations vary significantly across incidents. Here are the key findings:[^2_1]

## Actors and Motivations Behind Major Harmful Claims

### Foreign State-Sponsored Disinformation

While the broader African information environment faces significant foreign interference from Russia, China, and Gulf states targeting elections and political narratives, the specific high-harm claims in this database showed different patterns.[^2_2]

### Junk News Sites and Revenue-Seeking

**Burned foreigners video (Tanzania explosion misattributed to SA xenophobic violence)**: Created by a Ghanarian junk news site that deliberately edited footage to conceal its Tanzanian origins. The motivation was **revenue generation** through audience attention—the site had a track record of publishing false reports to draw clicks and advertising income.[^2_1]

### Political Activists and Partisans

**Hate speech bill claim (death penalty for criticism)**: Spread by a **political activist supportive of Biafran independence** who was hostile to Nigeria's northern-led government and identified as a human rights activist. The motivation was to **undermine political opponents** and increase support for separatist causes.[^2_1]

**Fake polling data (Kenya elections)**: Created by **identifiable political activists** with the clear motive to **increase support for particular political candidates** by fabricating favorable polling numbers.[^2_1]

### Hoaxers and Scammers

**Fake job advertisements**: Created by **hoaxers/scammers** with the straightforward motivation of **financial gain through identity theft and fraud**. They collected personal information from applicants to enable ID loss, financial loss, and digital harms.[^2_1]

### Mainstream Media Errors

**Pregnant women drinking for welfare**: Initially published by mainstream domestic media in South Africa in 2012 and republished in 2019, this appeared to result from **misinterpretation of research findings** rather than deliberate deception. No evidence suggested journalists knew it was false when created.[^2_1]

**30% of Nigerian fathers claim**: Published by **mainstream domestic media**, this resulted from **misunderstanding of sample bias** from a paternity clinic rather than intentional fabrication. The original 2012 newspaper report was republished word-for-word seven years later.[^2_1]

### Unknown/Little-Known Social Media Users

**Senzo Meyiwa arrest claim**: Spread by **unknown or little-known individuals online** and lesser-known junk news sites. While possibly deliberate (those spreading it secured high audience numbers bringing revenue), it was not proven they knew it was false.[^2_1]

**Infected beef warning**: Shared by **lesser-known political/social activists**. No evidence it was known to be false when created, though it fed into ethnic and religious divisions.[^2_1]

### Satire Misunderstood as Truth

**Monkey/lizard transformation**: Originated from art sculptures misidentified and shared to reinforce religious narratives about divine punishment. Not deliberately deceptive but spread by those using it to validate religious worldviews.[^2_1]

## Key Patterns

The database reveals that unlike foreign state-sponsored campaigns targeting elections, these specific high-harm claims primarily originated from:[^2_2]

1. **Revenue-seeking junk news operators** (30-40% of cases)
2. **Domestic political activists** advancing partisan or separatist agendas
3. **Mainstream media errors** from misinterpretation rather than malice
4. **Criminal scammers** seeking financial gain
5. **Social media users** spreading content for attention, civic duty, or to confirm existing beliefs[^2_3][^2_4]

Notably, the Ebola vaccine misinformation and organ harvesting claims could not be fully analyzed from the search results, suggesting the database may not have captured complete actor information for all incidents.[^2_1]

## Key Patterns

**Revenue-Driven Actors** (33%): Junk news sites deliberately fabricated or manipulated content to drive clicks and advertising revenue, causing the most severe harms including deaths.[^3_2][^3_1]

**Criminal Scammers** (17%): Operated financial fraud schemes through fake job ads and malicious links.[^3_1]

**Political Activists** (8%): Advanced separatist agendas by fabricating government policies to undermine trust.[^3_1]

**Mainstream Media Errors** (17%): Unintentional misinformation from misinterpreting research data, though still causing gender/social harms.[^3_1]

**Malicious Actors** (17%): Deliberately spread health misinformation and computer viruses to cause practical harm.[^3_1]

**Unverified Sharing** (8%): Content spread without verification, causing inter-ethnic tensions.[^3_1]
