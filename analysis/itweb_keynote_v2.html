The Taxonomy

Three Modes of Hybrid Attack

🎯
TYPE 1
Cyber-Enabled Influence
Cyber operation serves an influence goal. With or without intrusion (strict vs. broad reading).
Examples: Hack-and-leak, defacement, ransomware-as-coercion, CIB/fake accounts
🎭
TYPE 2
Influence-Enabled Cyber
Disinformation or deception enables the intrusion. The lie is the key to the door.
Examples: Vishing help desks, deepfake CFO fraud, spearphishing with false pretext, fraudulent client impersonation
⚡
TYPE 3
Parallel Operations
Same actor runs cyber and influence tracks simultaneously but independently. Amplifies strategic effect.
Examples: PRC pressure on Taiwan; Russian election interference 2016; Iran–Hamas operations

Global Landmarks

Four Cases That Changed the Playbook

🗳️DNC Hack-and-Leak · 2016

APT28 (GRU) spearphished Podesta → 50,000 emails → WikiLeaks/DCLeaks → election disruption. Type 1 textbook. 12 GRU officers indicted by Mueller.

🎬Sony Pictures · 2014

North Korea-linked GOP: months of access → wiper → 100TB leak → coercive threats → Sony pulled The Interview. Hack + destroy + coerce.

🎰MGM Resorts · 2023

Scattered Spider: 10-min LinkedIn recon → vishing help desk → Okta SSO → ALPHV ransomware → ~$100M losses. One call defeated MFA.

🤖Arup Deepfake · 2024

AI deepfake CFO video call → 15 wire transfers → $25.6M stolen. Zero system intrusion. The deception was the attack. "I saw them on camera."

Emerging Threat

AI is Supercharging the Hybrid Toolkit

🎭
Deepfake Fraud

Arup: $25.6M stolen via live deepfake video call. Built from LinkedIn + YouTube. Zero intrusion. "I saw them on camera."

🗣️
Voice Cloning / Vishing

3 seconds of audio = cloned voice. MGM Resorts: one vishing call defeated enterprise MFA and Okta SSO. $100M in losses.

🤖
AI-Scaled CIB

LLMs generate authentic-sounding content in any language. EEAS: AI enables simultaneous multi-language disinformation campaigns previously requiring native speakers.

🎯
AI-Personalised Spearphishing

Previous detection heuristics (grammar errors, generic greetings) no longer reliable. LLMs write perfect contextual lures from scraped social data.

Out-of-band verification (call-back on known number; in-person for large transfers) remains the most reliable countermeasure to AI impersonation

What To Do

Practical Response Playbook for Security Leaders

🔭 Detect the Hybrid
  • Wire SOC for narrative threats, not just technical IoCs
  • Monitor leak sites & dark web for brand mentions
  • Track social media for exec impersonation
🛡️ Defend the Human Layer
  • Help desk: callback on known number + manager approval for resets
  • Finance: multi-channel out-of-band for large transfers
  • Train staff to verify video calls independently
💪 Build Resilience
  • Offline backups + tested DR plan (Transnet model)
  • Segment OT/IT networks
  • Practice the ransomware scenario annually
🤝 Engage the Ecosystem
  • Notify Information Regulator within 72h (POPIA s.22)
  • Engage SABRIC, Hawks, AfricaCERT
  • Partner with civil society on manipulation/abuse tracks

Ransomware extortion is simultaneously a technical breach AND a psychological influence operation — your IR plan must address both tracks
