Framing the Problem

Four Ways to Weaponise Information & Communication

Focusing only on disinformation limits us to veracity-based countermeasures. The full attack surface is wider.

OASIS / STIX extension framing · All four quadrants require distinct countermeasures

The Taxonomy

Three Modes of Hybrid Attack

🎯
TYPE 1
Cyber-Enabled Influence
Cyber operation serves an influence goal. With or without intrusion (strict vs. broad).
Examples: Hack-and-leak, defacement, ransomware-as-coercion, CIB/fake accounts
🎭
TYPE 2
Influence-Enabled Cyber
Disinformation or deception enables the intrusion. The lie is the key.
Examples: Vishing help desks, deepfake CFO fraud, spearphishing, fraudulent client impersonation
⚡
TYPE 3
Parallel Operations
Same actor runs cyber and influence tracks simultaneously but independently.
Examples: PRC pressure on Taiwan; Russian election interference; Iran–Hamas operations

EEAS 4th Annual FIMI Report (2025): increased hybridisation is one of the top three global FIMI trends

When & Why

Trigger Conditions: When Each Attack Type Emerges

🗳️ Hack-and-Leak (1b)
  • Electoral contests with high stakes (presidential, EU-level)
  • Opposition figure or campaign as target
  • State actor with months of pre-positioning
  • Media ecosystem ready to amplify
💰 Ransomware / Double-Extortion (2f)
  • Exposed remote access (RDP, VPN, Citrix) with weak credentials
  • Absence of MFA on internet-facing systems
  • High-value data (credit records, health data, passenger PII)
  • Organisation reliant on continuous operations (ports, hospitals, food production)
📣 CIB / Fake Networks (1c)
  • Election periods (6-month window) — but now ongoing
  • Geopolitical contest for legitimacy (Sahel; BRICS)
  • Weak local platform moderation in target language
  • WhatsApp / closed-chain ecosystems with no fact-checking
🤖 Deepfake / Vishing (2b/2c)
  • Large wire-transfer authorisation workflows
  • Remote-work environments (reduced in-person verification)
  • Publicly available executive footage (LinkedIn, earnings calls)
  • No out-of-band verification protocol in place

AI is decoupling CIB operations from election calendars — continuous multi-language influence is now possible year-round

Global Landmarks

Four Cases That Changed the Playbook

🗳️DNC Hack-and-Leak · 2016

APT28 (GRU) spearphished Podesta → 50,000 emails → WikiLeaks/DCLeaks → election disruption. Type 1 textbook. 12 GRU officers indicted by Mueller.

🎬Sony Pictures · 2014

North Korea-linked GOP: months of access → wiper → 100TB leak → coercive threats → Sony pulled The Interview. Hack + destroy + coerce.

🎰MGM Resorts · 2023

Scattered Spider: 10-min LinkedIn recon → vishing help desk → Okta SSO → ALPHV ransomware → ~$100M losses. One call defeated MFA.

🤖Arup Deepfake · 2024

AI deepfake CFO video call → 15 wire transfers → $25.6M stolen. Zero system intrusion. The deception was the attack. "I saw them on camera."

Continental Pivot

Africa: A Different Operational Environment

📱Mobile-First Information Ecosystem

WhatsApp IS the public sphere for most Africans. Closed-chain forwarding defeats platform moderation. No fact-checking layer at scale.

🌐2,000+ Languages, Minimal Moderation

Content flagged in English in hours circulates in Zulu, Hausa, or Amharic for weeks. AI is now generating disinformation in these languages at scale.

📈Rapid Digital Adoption + Low Resilience

World's fastest-growing mobile internet market. Security infrastructure not keeping pace. Password 'Password' on a credit-bureau server is a structural feature, not an outlier.

🌍Geopolitical Contest

Russia–France contest in the Sahel. China's Belt and Road influence. US Africa Command. Each external actor runs both cyber and influence tracks simultaneously.

💼Commercial Influence-as-a-Service

Team Jorge targeted Nigeria, Senegal, Togo, Angola, Niger, Tunisia. Archimedes Group ran Facebook CIB across 6 African countries. This is a functioning market.

🏦High-Value, Under-Protected Targets

Telecoms (290M subscriber MTN breach), credit bureaux, ports, mining, aviation — all attacked by ransomware groups who see Africa as high-ROI and low-risk.

Response Framework

No One-Size-Fits-All Response

Each hybrid attack track requires a different institutional response. Mismatching response to threat type wastes resources and leaves real vulnerabilities unaddressed.

💀 Criminal Ransomware
Who responds: CISO, IR team, law enforcement (Hawks), Information Regulator
Legal hook: POPIA s.22 — notify regulator within 72 hours
Key tools: Offline backups, network segmentation, SABRIC, AfricaCERT
🌐 Foreign State Influence
Who responds: DIRCO, SSA, Electoral Commission, media, civil society
Legal hook: Electoral Act; RICA; proposed Online Safety Bill
Key tools: Platform engagement, prebunking, diplomatic attribution
🏠 Domestic CIB
Who responds: IEC, Press Ombudsman, SANEF, fact-checkers (Africa Check)
Legal hook: Electoral Code; ICASA; party funding disclosure
Key tools: Monitoring, media literacy, influencer transparency

Key insight: SA is unique in facing all three tracks simultaneously — which means it needs all three response frameworks operating in parallel. Most organisations are only resourced for one.

Global Governance

Multilateral Frameworks & Gaps

🇪🇺 EEAS / EDMO (EU)

Annual FIMI reports; Rapid Alert System for near-real-time disinformation threat sharing; EU AI Act (2025) first regulatory framework for AI-generated content labelling.

🌐 ITU / ITU-IMPACT

157 member countries. Provides threat intelligence, training, and incident response to developing nations. SA is a member. Global Cybersecurity Agenda framework.

🌍 African Union

Malabo Convention (2014): only 14 of 55 AU members ratified as of 2025. AU-CIRT established 2020. Major governance gap — continental framework exists but enforcement doesn't.

🔬 OASIS / STIX + FIRST

OASIS developing STIX extensions to model FIMI alongside traditional cyber threats. FIRST: 700+ global CERTs including AfricaCERT for regional incident coordination.

The gap: All major frameworks are primarily designed by Global North actors. African civil society (Paradigm Initiative, Research ICT Africa, CIPESA) is building continental capacity — but severely under-resourced relative to the threat scale.

For Security Leaders

What Firms Can Do

🔭 Detect the Hybrid
  • Wire SOC for narrative threats, not just technical IoCs
  • Monitor leak sites & dark web for brand/exec mentions
  • Track social media for executive impersonation
🛡️ Defend the Human Layer
  • Help desk: callback on known number + manager approval
  • Finance: multi-channel out-of-band for large transfers
  • Train staff: one call to a known number saves millions
💪 Build Resilience
  • Offline backups + tested DR plan (Transnet model)
  • Segment OT/IT networks
  • Annual ransomware tabletop exercises
🤝 Engage the Ecosystem
  • Notify Information Regulator within 72h (POPIA s.22)
  • Engage SABRIC, Hawks, AfricaCERT, SAFPS
  • Plan crisis comms for the leak-site narrative threat

Ransomware extortion is simultaneously a technical breach AND a psychological influence operation — your IR plan must address both tracks

Civil Society & Citizens

What Communities Can Do

🧠
Prebunking > Fact-Checking

Inoculation reduces susceptibility by 20–30%. Must be in local languages: Zulu, Xhosa, Sotho, Hausa, Swahili — not English only.

👥
Trusted Community Messengers

Health disinformation (COVID, polio) countered most effectively by religious leaders and community health workers — not government spokespeople. Same for political disinformation.

📱
Platform Accountability

Media Monitoring Africa, Right2Know successfully pressured platforms. Meta ending 3rd-party fact-checking affects Africa Check (SA-headquartered). Advocate for African-language moderation investment.

🔐
Digital Rights & Privacy

Register with SAFPS if data breached. Teach communities: what to do if your ID is in a breach. SIM-swap fraud is a direct consequence of ransomware group data sales.

🗳️
Election Integrity Monitoring

EISA, SADC Election Support Network, IEC need digital/cyber threat monitoring integrated with traditional election observation. Report CIB activity to platforms and IEC.

🔬
Build Local Research Capacity

Paradigm Initiative, CIPESA, Research ICT Africa building continental capacity. Under-resourced relative to the threat. Support and fund African researchers studying African hybrid threats.
